Essential Information

Everything you need to know before your trip

Location & Size

Boracay is a small island in Western Visayas, Philippines, about 7 kilometers long and 1 kilometer wide at its narrowest point. The main attraction is White Beach, a 4‐kilometer stretch of powdery white sand on the western side.

Best Time to Visit

Dry Season (November to May): Best weather, calm seas, ideal for swimming and water sports. Peak season is December to April.

Wet Season (June to October): More rain and stronger winds. Hotels offer lower rates, but some water activities may be limited.

How to Get There

1. Fly to Caticlan Airport (MPH) ‐ Closest option, 10 minutes to jetty port

2. Fly to Kalibo International Airport (KLO) ‐ Cheaper flights, 1.5‐2 hours by van to jetty port

From the jetty port, take a 15-minute boat ride to Boracay. Upon arrival, pay the environmental fee and tourism tax.

Popular Activities

  • Island hopping (Crystal Cove, Crocodile Island)
  • Snorkeling and scuba diving
  • Sunset sailing on traditional paraw boats
  • Kiteboarding and windsurfing at Bulabog Beach
  • Cliff diving at Ariel's Point
  • Visit Puka Shell Beach and Mt. Luho viewpoint

Important Rules

  • No smoking on the beach (designated areas only)
  • No single-use plastics allowed
  • No drinking alcohol on the beach
  • No building sandcastles or digging large holes
  • Cliff diving at Ariel's Point
  • Respect environmental fees and regulations

Practical Tips

Currency: Philippine Peso (PHP). ATMs available, but bring cash for small vendors.

Transportation: Tricycles are the main transport around the island. Agree on fare before riding.

Internet: Most hotels and restaurants have WiFi. Local SIM cards available at the airport.

Safety: Generally safe for tourists. Drink bottled water and use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the marine life..
